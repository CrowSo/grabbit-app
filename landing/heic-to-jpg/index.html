What is HEIC and why can't Windows open it by default?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's default photo format on iPhone and iPad since iOS 11. It offers excellent image quality at about half the file size of JPG. The problem is that Windows 10 and 11 don't natively support HEIC — when you transfer iPhone photos to your PC, they appear as unrecognized files that Windows Photos can't open without a paid codec pack from the Microsoft Store.

The simplest fix is to convert HEIC photos to JPG, which every Windows app can open. Grabbit's local Converter does this without uploading your photos to any server — the conversion runs entirely on your PC.
